Recognizing Excellence:

Our Achievements in Law.

Lex Mundi Pro Bono Foundation Award.

Udo Udoma & Belo-Osagie is delighted to have been a recipient of the Lex Mundi Pro Bono Foundation Award together with other awardees, Clarke Gittens Farmer, Davis Graham & Stubbs LLP, Pestalozzi Attorneys at Law, and Romulo Law Offices, our fellow Lex Mundi member firms at the 14th Annual Lex Mundi Pro Bono Foundation Award which was held in San Francisco on Thursday, 4 May 2023. Our firm was lauded for our exceptional pro bono contributions and support in providing critical pro bono legal services and support to social impact organisation.

Our Managing Partner, Aniekan Ukpanah, who received the award on behalf of the firm, notes that at UUBO, pro bono service is an integral part of our firm's culture because we believe that we have a vital role to play in strengthening and developing our nation and society; and one of the ways we can achieve this is through our pro bono work that supports various non-profits and social enterprises who are playing an important role in improving the quality of lives of millions of Nigerians. Congratulations to our pro bono team, partners, and lawyers, who worked on various pro bono matters.

For more information about our pro bono practice and other practice area service offerings, please get in touch with us at probono@uubo.org and uubo@uubo.org, and visit our website at https://lnkd.in/gTPDxr_g.

TrustLaw Collaboration Award.

Udo Udoma & Belo-Osagie is honoured to have been nominated for the TrustLaw Collaboration Award, one of the awards to be presented at the 2023 TrustLaw Awards organised by the Thomson Reuters Foundation. The Collaboration Award showcases highly effective working relationships between pro bono providers and recipients that exemplify the power and best practices of pro bono.

UUBO was nominated for its collaborative work with counsel in Colombia (Philippi Prietocarrioza Ferrero DU & Uría Colombia), Kenya (ALN), Uganda (TASLAF Advocates and Consultants), Zambia (Mweshi Banda & Associates), Brazil (Machado, Meyer Sendacz e Opice) and India (F5 Networks, Inc) on a comparative research project on firearm ownership and safety laws for Gun Free South Africa. The research project focused on the legal framework of firearm ownership and safety laws, as part of Gun Free South Africa's objective to strengthen gun control policy in South Africa. The 2023 TrustLaw Awards will take place on May 16th 2023 in London and will celebrate remarkable pro bono projects undertaken by legal teams with NGOs and social enterprises around the world.

UUBO is proud to support the efforts of Gun Free South Africa and similar organisations working tirelessly to reduce gun violence. For more information about Udo Udoma & Belo-Osagie's pro bono and full service offerings, please email us at probono@uubo.org and visit www.uubo.org.

IFLR Africa Awards 2022.

UUBO has performed exceptionally in this year’s IFLR Africa Awards 2022, receiving a total of 5 awards across 3 jurisdictional and 2 deal categories, namely: Nigeria – Law Firm of the Year; Loans Firm of the Year; M&A Firm of the Year; Loans Deal of the Year; and M&A Deal of the Year.

The #IFLRAfricaAwards provide a unique opportunity to celebrate leading firms and individuals behind the most innovative and challenging legal work. The @IFLR1000 states that, in selecting category winners for these awards, and in addition to reviewing the role of each individual firm in the deal, it looks for new legal structures, deals that overcome new regulatory hurdles, and regional ‘firsts’ that ‘adapt existing technology to local regulation’, assessing the ‘’intricacy of that innovation…as well as likely market impact’’. A full list of categories and shortlists for the @IFLR1000 Africa Awards 2022 is available to view here: https://lnkd.in/dF8H5APi

We are grateful to the @IFLR, our multi-jurisdictional collaborators, and especially for the continuing trust of our clients. We are also delighted that our teams’ depth of talent, consistent hard work, and dedication continue to be independently recognized and celebrated.
